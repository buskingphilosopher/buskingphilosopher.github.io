Music Analysis

The idea behind the style of writing about music you'll find below is to prove that art can be objective. For too long people have belittled the arts with claims that there's no right and wrong, that it's all just reports of subjective opinions. Naturally, there is a subjective component to art. I don't deny this. What I deny is that there isn't also an objective component. And if you don't believe me, have a read of the pieces below.

The aim is to talk only about what is in the piece. Not what I bring to the piece. No talk of how the symphony reminded me of a crashing wave or a barron landscape. I make insights only where there is evidence in the piece for it, and hence any claim I make is vulnerable to your criticism. If I've made mistakes, feel free to correct me and we can arrive at an answer by consulting the manuscript. This keeps the discussion free of unfalsifiable claims and demonstrates that art doesn't always have to be the reporting of subjective claims none of us are entitled to contradict. Instead, we are engaged in identifying real facts 'out there' which we can meaningfully disagree about. And that's quite an underexplored approach to art at the moment. Not the only legitimate approach, but certainly one of a selection of legitimate methodologies for approaching the arts.
