Sonnet 5

Structure & Context
Form: Shakespearean sonnet — ABAB CDCD EFEF GG; iambic pentameter throughout Sequence: Fifth procreation sonnet; paired with Sonnet 6, which applies the distillation argument directly to reproduction Argument: Time destroys external beauty; distillation — capturing the essence of summer in perfume — is offered as the model for what a child does with a parent's beauty Two-part structure: Lines 1–8 describe Time's destruction of summer; lines 9–14 introduce distillation as the counter-argument, via a conditional hypothetical resolved by 'but' Key distinction: 'Show' (external appearance) versus 'substance' (inner essence) — the closing couplet names the two things Time can and cannot destroy Context: The distillation of flowers into perfume was well understood in Elizabethan England; it was a process specifically associated with preserving summer's fragrance through winter

Those hours, that with gentle work did frame The lovely gaze where every eye doth dwell, Will play the tyrants to the very same And that unfair which fairly doth excel: For never-resting time leads summer on To hideous winter and confounds him there, Sap checked with frost and lusty leaves quite gone, Beauty o'ersnowed and bareness every where: Then, were not summer's distillation left, A liquid prisoner pent in walls of glass, Beauty's effect with beauty were bereft, Nor it nor no remembrance what it was: But flowers distill'd though they with winter meet, Leese but their show; their substance still lives sweet.
