Analytic Poetry

After studying analytic philosophy at university, I rediscovered a strange book from which I was taught poetry in sixth form on something called 'analytic poetry'. His name was Nicholas Marsh and he authored a Routledge series he called 'Analysing Texts,' applying analytic methods to Blake, Shakespeare, Larkin and others. His belief was that poetry lent itself to precise analysis in just the way science did. I knew that philosophy, also a 'fuzzy' humanities subject, had surprisingly benefited from embracing analytic methods, and I became fascinated by its possibility for new ways of thinking about poetry.

But no one really took notice of Marsh's ideas during his life, and those who did were furious at the attempt to apply mathematical procedures to poetry. To them, the beauty of poetry would disintegrate if subjected to analysis, the way a joke loses its humour when explained. While I sympathise with this criticism, I think it's misguided and unfair. This webpage is devoted to my attempts to continue the philosphical vision which Marsh inaugurated, and do justice to his methods (which I believe are capable of generating insights which can illuminate the magic of poetry) while at the same time acknowledging that this is by no means the only way to approach a poem.
