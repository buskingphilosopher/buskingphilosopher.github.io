THE TRADING
GAME
A confession in five rounds

You are sitting in a conference room at LSE. Around you: economics students from Oxford, Cambridge, UCL. Blazers. Calculators. Complete confidence that they know how this works.

Citibank is watching. The prize is an internship — a golden ticket to the trading floor at Canary Wharf. The winner gets the job. Everyone else goes home.

The game: a deck of numbered cards. Each player holds one face-down. Three community cards are dealt face-down and revealed one at a time. You are all betting on the total sum of all cards. You do this by making two-way prices — bid and ask — just like a real trader.

The others will play it as a maths problem. You can play it as something else entirely.

◈ How It Works
1.You are dealt a card. You can see your own number. Nobody else can.
2.Three community cards are dealt face-down. One is flipped per phase.
3.Everyone simultaneously posts a bid / ask on what they think the total will be. Your bid is what you'll buy at; your ask is what you'll sell at.
4.You can hit another player's bid (sell to them at their bid price) or lift their ask (buy from them at their ask price). Trades are for 1–3 units.
5.When all cards are revealed, the true total is calculated. P&L = your net position × (true total − your average trade price).
6.The player with the most profit after 5 rounds gets the internship.
