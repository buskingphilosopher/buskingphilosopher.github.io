Satan's Soliloquy on Mount Niphates

John Milton — Paradise Lost, Book IV, lines 32–113 (1667)
Hover or click highlighted passages to read annotations
Structure & Context
Form: Blank verse — unrhymed iambic pentameter; Milton's chosen epic vehicle Occasion: Satan, alone for the first time, faces the Sun before entering Eden — no audience, no performance Dramatic irony: The reader has seen Satan perform defiance in Books I–II; here we see behind the performance Movement: Three phases — apostrophe to the Sun (32–57), self-interrogation (58–78), failed repentance and resolution (79–113) Central paradox: The soliloquy begins as rhetoric and breaks down into logic — and the logic destroys Satan's position Context: Milton wrote Paradise Lost blind, dictating it; the soliloquy is widely read as his most psychologically modern passage

32O thou that with surpassing Glory crown'd, 33Look'st from thy sole Dominion like the God 34Of this new World; at whose sight all the Starrs 35Hide thir diminisht heads; to thee I call, 36But with no friendly voice, and add thy name 37O Sun, to tell thee how I hate thy beams 38That bring to my remembrance from what state 39I fell, how glorious once above thy Spheare; 40Till Pride and worse Ambition threw me down 41Warring in Heav'n against Heav'ns matchless King: 51The debt immense of endless gratitude, 52So burdensome still paying, still to ow; 66Thou hadst: whom hast thou then or what to accuse, 67But Heav'ns free Love dealt equally to all? 68Be then his Love accurst, since love or hate, 69To me alike, it deals eternal woe. 70Nay curs'd be thou; since against his thy will 71Chose freely what it now so justly rues. 72Me miserable! which way shall I fly 73Infinite wrath, and infinite despair? 74Which way I fly is Hell; my self am Hell; 75And in the lowest deep a lower deep 76Still threatning to devour me opens wide, 77To which the Hell I suffer seems a Heav'n. 78O then at last relent: is there no place 79Left for Repentance, none for Pardon left? 80None left but by submission; and that word 81Disdain forbids me, and my dread of shame 82Among the Spirits beneath, whom I seduc'd 88While they adore me on the Throne of Hell, 89With Diadem and Sceptre high advanc'd 90The lower still I fall, onely Supream 91In miserie; such joy Ambition finds. 92But say I could repent and could obtaine 93By Act of Grace my former state; how soon 94Would higth recall high thoughts, how soon unsay 95What feign'd submission swore: ease would recant 96Vows made in pain, as violent and void. 107So farewel Hope, and with Hope farewel Fear, 108Farewel Remorse: all Good to me is lost; 109Evil, be thou my Good; by thee at least 110Divided empire with Heav'ns King I hold, 111By thee, and more than half perhaps will reign; 112As Man ere long, and this new World shall know.
